Course presentation
This course provides an accessible introduction to the key concepts of economics and the fundamental tools used to understand how economies work. Topics include supply and demand analysis, how prices are determined, different types of markets, how we measure economic performance (such as GDP and inflation), the causes of economic booms and recessions, and the role of government and central banks in managing the economy

Criterios de evaluación

The final grade is the weigthed average of:
  • Midterm exams: 40% of the final grade
  • Final exam: 60% of the final grade
There will be 4 midterm exams during the course. The best 3 midterm exams will count towards the final grade. Each midterm exam will cover the material taught since the previous midterm exam (or since the beginning of the course, for the first midterm exam). The midterm exams will consist of multiple-choice questions and short-answer questions.
To pass the course, it is necessary to score 5 or higher on the final exam. If the student fails the final exam, the final mark to be included in the student’s record will be the final exam mark out of 10. If the student does not take the final exam or is unable to take the final exam because the required percentage of attendance has not been achieved, the final exam mark will be zero. This criterion applies to both regular and extraordinary examinations.
